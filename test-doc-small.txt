**Overview**
Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players on a circular or oval field. At the centre is a 22-yard pitch with a wicket at each end. The batting team scores runs while the fielding team tries to dismiss batters and restrict scoring. An innings ends when ten batters are dismissed. Formats range from five-day Test matches to the fast-paced Twenty20 game that lasts three to four hours.

**History**
Cricket originated as a children's game in medieval south-east England, with the earliest definite reference dating to 1597 in Guildford. It grew into a professional sport through the 18th century, fuelled by gambling and aristocratic patronage. The Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC) was formed in 1787 and became the custodian of the Laws. The sport spread globally through the British Empire, with the first Test match played between England and Australia in 1876-77. The famous Ashes rivalry began in 1882. Limited overs cricket was introduced in England in 1963, and the first Cricket World Cup was held in 1975.

**How the Game Works**
A coin toss decides which team bats first. Batters score runs by hitting the ball and running between wickets, or by hitting the ball to the boundary for an automatic four runs, or over the boundary for six. An over consists of six legal deliveries bowled by one player. Batters can be dismissed in nine ways, the most common being bowled, caught, leg before wicket, run out, and stumped. Two umpires officiate on the field, with a third umpire available for video reviews in televised matches.

**Equipment and Clothing**
The bat is made of white willow, no wider than 4.25 inches and no longer than 38 inches. The ball is a hard leather-seamed spheroid with a cork core, delivered at speeds exceeding 145 km/h. Batters and wicket-keepers wear protective gear including pads, gloves, helmets, and body padding. Traditional cricket kit is all white, though limited overs cricket uses team colours.

**Player Roles**
Teams include specialist batters, bowlers, all-rounders, and a wicket-keeper. Bowlers are classified as fast bowlers, seam bowlers, or spinners. Fast bowlers rely on pace and swing; spinners rely on guile and deception. The captain is typically the most experienced player and makes tactical decisions on field placement and bowling changes. Bowlers cannot bowl consecutive overs, so at least two bowlers alternate each over.

**Formats**
Test cricket is the highest standard, played over five days with each team having two innings. First-class cricket follows a similar structure at the domestic level. One Day Internationals allow 50 overs per side and must be completed in a single day. Twenty20 gives each team 20 overs and is designed to finish in an evening. Even shorter formats like T10 (10 overs) and The Hundred (100 balls) have emerged recently, though not without controversy.

**Governance**
The International Cricket Council (ICC), headquartered in Dubai, governs the sport globally with 105 member nations, 12 of which are full Test-playing members. The ICC organises major tournaments including the Cricket World Cup, T20 World Cup, and ICC Champions Trophy. Each member nation has its own cricket board managing domestic competitions and the national team.

**Women's Cricket**
Women's cricket was first recorded in Surrey in 1745. The first women's Test match was played between Australia and England in 1934. The International Women's Cricket Council merged with the ICC in 2005, and women's cricket has grown significantly in the 21st century with T20 International status awarded to all ICC members.

**Cultural Impact**
Cricket has deeply influenced the culture and language of Commonwealth nations, giving rise to phrases like "that's not cricket" (meaning unfair) and "on a sticky wicket" (meaning a difficult situation). The sport has inspired literature, painting, and music, and has historical connections to the development of Australian rules football and baseball's scoring system.

**Global Reach**
Cricket's fanbase has crossed one billion people, with 90% based in South Asia. The Indian Premier League has become one of the most valuable sports leagues in the world, and T20 cricket is set to appear in the 2028 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles.
